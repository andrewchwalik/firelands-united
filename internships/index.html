Internships

Thanks for your interest in joining Firelands United. Explore the internship tracks below, read through each role carefully, and apply for the one that best matches your strengths, interests, and goals within the club.

Event Operations Internship

This internship is designed for students who want practical sports operations experience in a live match environment. Interns learn how to plan, execute, and evaluate event logistics while supporting the fan experience and operational standards of Firelands United.

Core Responsibilities

  • Support pre-match setup, post-match teardown, and field-side operational checklists
  • Assist with fan entry flow, signage setup, hospitality coordination, and venue readiness
  • Help coordinate volunteer assignments, radio/phone communication flow, and issue tracking
  • Contribute to post-match debrief notes and action items for the next event

Learning Outcomes

  • Build professional event planning, execution, and contingency-management skills
  • Develop communication and leadership habits in a high-tempo team environment
  • Learn how operational decisions impact players, staff, and supporters on match day
  • Gain transferable project-management and stakeholder-coordination experience

Internship Track (10-12 Weeks)

Phase 1 (Weeks 1-2): Orientation, operations handbook review, venue walkthrough, and shadowing current match-day workflows.

Phase 2 (Weeks 3-5): Shared execution ownership for setup, fan-flow stations, and volunteer communication under close supervision.

Phase 3 (Weeks 6-9): Lead one operational workstream each match day (for example: check-in, signage, or fan zone execution).

Phase 4 (Weeks 10-12): Deliver a final event improvement recommendation with measurable process upgrades.

Required Deliverables

  • Weekly operations log with priorities, issues, and improvement notes
  • One updated match-day checklist or SOP improvement draft
  • Final presentation of recommended operational improvements

Supervision: Assigned staff mentor with weekly feedback checkpoint and midpoint/final evaluation.

Typical Commitment: 8-15 hours per week in-season (including match-day hours).

Marketing Internship

This internship gives students direct experience in sports marketing and club communications. Interns help plan, create, and publish content that supports fan growth, sponsor value, and club storytelling across the season.

Core Responsibilities

  • Contribute to weekly content calendar planning for social, web, and match promotions
  • Assist with photo/video capture plans, caption writing, and post scheduling
  • Support sponsor spotlights, campaign concepts, and game-week creative execution
  • Track basic content metrics and report what is performing best

Learning Outcomes

  • Develop a sports-focused marketing workflow from ideation to published deliverable
  • Learn audience-first content strategy and campaign optimization fundamentals
  • Build communication, collaboration, and brand consistency habits
  • Create portfolio-ready work that demonstrates real campaign impact

Internship Track (10-12 Weeks)

Phase 1 (Weeks 1-2): Brand standards onboarding, channel review, and content workflow training.

Phase 2 (Weeks 3-5): Co-create weekly social plans and support content production for match-week storytelling.

Phase 3 (Weeks 6-9): Own a mini-campaign (for example: player feature, match-week push, or sponsor spotlight).

Phase 4 (Weeks 10-12): Present campaign outcomes, insights, and next-step recommendations.

Required Deliverables

  • Weekly content plan with captions and creative direction notes
  • At least one completed campaign recap with performance observations
  • End-of-term portfolio packet of selected social/creative work

Supervision: Assigned marketing lead with weekly review session and midpoint/final evaluation.

Typical Commitment: 6-12 hours per week with flexible planning blocks around matches.

Coaching Internship

This internship is structured for aspiring coaches who want hands-on experience in session planning support, training operations, and player development systems. Interns work closely with staff to connect coaching theory to practical execution.

Core Responsibilities

  • Assist with session setup, field organization, and equipment readiness
  • Support player check-in, attendance tracking, and training logistics
  • Observe and document key coaching points, session outcomes, and team trends
  • Provide support tasks during training and match preparation under staff direction

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand how to structure effective training environments and coaching workflows
  • Build communication skills with players, coaches, and operational staff
  • Develop observational analysis habits for player and session evaluation
  • Gain applied experience for future coaching licenses and pathways

Internship Track (10-12 Weeks)

Phase 1 (Weeks 1-2): Orientation to club coaching model, training standards, and session support expectations.

Phase 2 (Weeks 3-5): Support core training logistics and begin structured observation reporting.

Phase 3 (Weeks 6-9): Take ownership of selected support functions and present weekly coaching observations.

Phase 4 (Weeks 10-12): Submit a final coaching reflection with practical recommendations.

Required Deliverables

  • Weekly training observation log
  • Session support checklist completion and progression notes
  • Final reflection report on coaching growth, insights, and recommendations

Supervision: Assigned coaching mentor with weekly feedback touchpoint and midpoint/final evaluation.

Typical Commitment: 8-14 hours per week based on training and match schedule.
