Amelia Chartrant Becomes the First Woman to Sign in Firelands United History

Firelands United is proud to announce Amelia Chartrant as the first woman to sign in club history, a milestone moment for both the player and the growing game in the Firelands.

For Chartrant, the opportunity is about much more than continuing her own career. It is also about showing the next generation what is possible.

“Being the first female to sign for Firelands United means to me that all my hard work through the years has paid off,” Chartrant said. “When I first heard about Firelands United, I wanted to join so I could continue playing soccer at a higher level. Now I want to use this opportunity to show young girls in the community the opportunities there are for you in sports as you get older, and that playing sports as an adult doesn't mean you have to play in college.”

When she first learned the club was building a women’s team, Chartrant immediately saw the impact it could have, not only for her, but for the entire region.

“When I first heard about Firelands United creating a women’s team, I saw it as not only a great opportunity for myself but for the community,” she said. “Bringing high-level soccer to the Sandusky/Norwalk area is huge. I’ve been playing soccer since I was 8 years old, and I would have loved to watch a women’s team in the area. I’m hoping the club and myself will inspire young athletes in the area.”

Chartrant’s soccer journey began at age 8. After one season with Edison Youth Soccer, she joined Edge FC, now Greater Firelands FC, where she was coached by Nico Samaniego from her very first day in club soccer.

“He has taught me almost everything I know about not only soccer but also leadership,” Chartrant said.

She went on to build an impressive career at Edison High School, where she was a four-year varsity starter, a four-year letter winner, earned multiple district and conference honors, and was named All-Ohio as a senior.

After graduation, Chartrant was set to continue her career at Heidelberg University. But after making it through preseason training, she was forced to step away and undergo hip surgery, a difficult moment that ultimately led her to Firelands United.

That challenge was years in the making. Chartrant was born with a bone deformity in her hips, though she did not discover it until she was around 12 years old. For years, she dealt with severe joint and muscle pain while continuing to play.

“Some days it forced me to sit on the bench,” she said. “Having to sit on the bench gave me a better understanding of how to support my team from the side, allowing myself to become more well-rounded as a captain. Being able to support your team on the field and off is what makes you a great leader and captain.”

Eventually, the joint deformity caused her labrum to tear, leading to the surgery that changed the course of her career.

“For the first time in my life, I walked away from something I committed to,” Chartrant said. “But this experience taught me that you have to take care of yourself, even if that means walking away from something you have worked years for. I also learned that everything happens for a reason, and if I wouldn't have left and had surgery, I wouldn't get the great opportunity to better myself and the community with Firelands United.”

Firelands United Chief Soccer Officer Andrew Chwalik said Chartrant represents exactly the kind of player and person the club wants to build around.

“Amelia is a tremendous player, but just as importantly, she brings resilience, leadership, and a genuine passion for growing the game in our community,” Chwalik said. “Having her become the first woman to sign in club history is a special moment for Firelands United, and we believe she will inspire a lot of young players across the region.”

On the field, Chartrant describes herself in three words: tenacious, technical, and calm.

That combination should give Firelands fans plenty to look forward to.

“When I play, I am very much a team player,” she said. “I rarely dribble down the field on my own. I prefer to move with my midfield and forwards and complete a lot of give-and-go’s along the way. I also prefer to ‘shoot’ from within the box going one-on-one with the keeper. I see the shot more as just a pass into the goal.”

Off the field, Chartrant’s personality comes through just as clearly. Her pregame music choice leans heavy.

“Having to choose just one artist or song is extremely difficult for me, but one song I listen to every pregame is ‘Bratva’ by Slaughter to Prevail,” she said. “I prefer heavy metal for my pregame playlist, and everyday listening.”

As for the postgame routine, she keeps it simple.

“Personally, I hate eating after games, especially in the summer,” Chartrant said. “Typically I’ll have a Gatorade and some beef jerky. If I were to eat, it would most definitely be something light, such as a salad or wrap.”

As Firelands United continues building its women’s side, Chartrant’s signing is more than a first. It is a statement about the future of the club and the opportunities it hopes to create across the region.
