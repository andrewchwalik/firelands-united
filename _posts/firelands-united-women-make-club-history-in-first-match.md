Firelands United’s women’s first team made club history on Saturday, May 16, playing the first women’s match in Firelands United history against Süsserfuss-Ballverein.

The final score was a difficult one, with Süsserfuss-Ballverein earning a 17-0 win after taking a 6-0 lead into halftime. But for Firelands United, the day represented something much bigger than the result.

This was the beginning of a new chapter for the club and for women’s soccer in the Firelands region.

Firelands entered the match with a small roster and only two substitutes. Several players had only recently returned to the area from college, and nearly half the team had not met before stepping into the opening stretch of the season together. Against an established Süsserfuss side with a deep bench, strong quality across the field, and a long club history behind them, it was always going to be a challenging first opponent.

The conditions added another layer of difficulty, with the match played on a waterlogged pitch that made rhythm and consistency hard to find.

Still, the importance of the day was not defined by the scoreboard.

For years, there has been a lack of investment in the women’s game across the Firelands region. This team was created to help change that. The first match was rough, but it was also a necessary first step toward building something lasting: a program that gives local players a place to compete, grow, and represent their community.

*“No one wants to be on the wrong end of a scoreline like that, but this match was never only about one result,”* said Andrew Chwalik, head coach of the Firelands United women’s first team. *“This was about starting. It was about giving women in our region a team to play for and giving young girls something to look at and say, ‘That could be me someday.’ We are building a foundation, and foundations are not always pretty at the beginning. But they matter. This matters.”*

Süsserfuss-Ballverein showed the standard Firelands United is working toward. Their experience, depth, and quality were clear throughout the match, and they punished mistakes with clinical finishing.

For Firelands United, the focus now turns to growth. The first match gave the team a starting point, and every training session and match from here will be part of building the identity, culture, and competitive standard of the women’s program.

The result will sting, but the moment will remain important.

Firelands United’s women’s first team has officially taken the field. The foundation has been laid. And the work to build the future of women’s soccer in the Firelands region has begun.
